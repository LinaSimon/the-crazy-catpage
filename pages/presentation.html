Q&A

1. What is your project and why did you choose to do it?

I chose to create a framework for a future portfolio because I thought it would be handy to have started building it already.

2. What is the biggest problem you faced building your site and how did you solve it?

Except from the JS fetching the random cats, it was definitely trying to make the site responsive. When you change something in the style, something else crashes and it seems to go on and on forever…

3. How did you plan your project and how did you follow that plan?

I found a website I liked vitaarchitecture.com and used as inspiration but of course my site doesn’t look anything like it. Maybe someday…. :D
I didn’t follow my plan at all! It was boring not to have any content so - the portfolio turned into a silly mini site with cats, which was more fun.

4. What are you most proud of in your project?

I’m most proud of the JS. I think it’s ok for a beginner even though I got help from a friend. :)

5. What are your greatest learnings doing this project?

My greatest learning is that planning is essential. It takes so much time to just follow your feelings and try stuff out with no real plan. It was ok though, because I needed to explore and try out a million things, to be able to learn stuff. Also, to NAME classes and id:s in a logical way. And to use sass.
